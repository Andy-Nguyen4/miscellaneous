Neuschwanstein Castle was commissioned to be built by King Ludwig II. The castle combines elements from various architectural styles, including Gothic, Romanesque, and Byzantine, to reflect the romanticist vision of royalty and art held by King Ludwig II.

Where?

On a rugged hill above the village of Hohenschwangau. The village is at the southern tip of the state of Bavaria, Germany. It is near the Austrian border and lie at the foothills of the Alpes.
